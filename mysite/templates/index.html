Making A Budget

How To Get Started

Steps To Making a Budget

The first step is to track your expenses and income.

You can download your debit or credit card details to see where your expenses are. You will want to categorize each expense into categories like Entertainment, Utilities, Rent, Restaurants, Shopping, Automobile, Insurance, Phone, etc. The most crucial part is understanding where you spend your money, including on things like Starbucks or buying lunch out at work every day. Those small expenses can add up quickly and consume a lot of money, even if it does not seem like it.

Steps To Making a Budget Continued

Now you put all your expenses and income into the budgeting spreadsheet to total up.

Different strategies include designating 50% of your monthly income to needs, 30% to your wants, and 20% to savings. Understandably, some people cannot afford to save that much money every month, so that you can adjust those percentages for your own budget. It is possible that you cannot save much money at the end of each month with all your monthly bills. The important part is trying to save anything you can, as it will eventually add up to a large sum over the years.
